Ryu ships three devices. All three are ESP32-S3 thin clients; they differ in display, sensors, and role. The watch is chat-first with a round screen, the necklace is ambient-first with no screen, and the desk hub is an always-on dashboard panel.

## Watch

A wrist device with a small round color display for on-demand chat and opportunistic ambient capture.

- **Chip** - ESP32-S3 (8 MB PSRAM). Reference build: a bare ESP32-S3 plus a GC9A01 1.28-inch round IPS LCD (240x240, SPI), or an integrated board such as the LILYGO T-Watch S3.
- **Display** - the round LCD runs an LVGL UI: a simplified island face driven by the `emotion` message, the live transcript from `stt`, and the streamed chat reply. It is the small-screen analog of the desktop island eyes. The watch also gains a dashboard view mode you can switch to with the button.
- **Audio** - I2S mic plus amplifier (for example INMP441 + MAX98357A, or an ES8311 codec).
- **Role** - on-demand voice chat (wake word or button, stream, reply, TTS) plus ambient capture when it is relayed through a phone.

## Necklace

An audio-first pendant with no display, designed to be the primary 24/7 ambient memory device.

- **Chip** - ESP32-S3 with an I2S mic, and an optional OV2640 camera.
- **No display** - its output surfaces elsewhere: a phone, a paired watch, or the desk hub. The necklace itself is a sensor plus optional voice in and out.
- **Camera (optional)** - enabling the OV2640 path captures stills or low-frame-rate JPEG on a trigger or interval, not continuous video, and streams them to a vision-capable agent. The camera needs PSRAM and costs battery, so it is event-driven.
- **Role** - the always-on ambient device. It streams Opus over BLE to the relay and into the meetings chunk pipeline. On-device VAD is **mandatory** here, both for battery and for ASR cost.

## Desk hub

An always-on, always-plugged desk panel built around e-ink for glanceable information.

- **Chip** - ESP32-S3, with a Waveshare 7.5-inch e-ink panel (UC8179, 800x480) and an optional ST7789 LCD.
- **E-ink primary surface** - a TRMNL-style dashboard: clock, agenda, weather, the rolling ambient summary, the last message. It updates on a poll interval with partial refresh and periodic full clears. E-ink cannot animate or stream tokens, so it shows finished output and holds it. See [Dashboard](/docs/hardware/dashboard).
- **Optional LCD** - a secondary ST7789 can run the live island face and chat over the WebSocket.
- **Role** - always-on ambient display and interactive hub. Because it is plugged in, it is always Mode B.

<Callout type="info">
  A Raspberry Pi 5 is **not** the desk device. The desk device is an ESP32-S3 thin client like the others. A Pi 5 can optionally be the *node host* that the desk (and every other device) connects to, giving local inference at the desk. See [Deployment](/docs/hardware/deployment).
</Callout>

## Comparison

| | Watch | Necklace | Desk hub |
|---|---|---|---|
| Chip | ESP32-S3 | ESP32-S3 | ESP32-S3 |
| Display | Round LCD (GC9A01) | None | E-ink (UC8179) + optional LCD |
| Camera | No | Optional (OV2640) | No |
| Primary role | On-demand chat | 24/7 ambient | Ambient dashboard |
| Power | Battery | Battery | Wall-powered |
| Typical mode | Mode B or A | Mode A (relay) | Mode B (WiFi) |
| Dashboard surface | Yes (view mode) | No | Yes (primary) |

## The 24/7 battery reality

The always-on constraint is what shapes the wearables, and it is a physics problem, not a tuning problem.

- A wearable streaming **direct over WiFi** lasts roughly 1 to 4 hours. WiFi transmit (on the order of 150 mA) is the killer.
- A wearable streaming Opus **over BLE to a phone relay** lasts far longer, on the order of 10 to 14 hours, because BLE draws a fraction of the power. This is exactly why Ryu uses a relay for always-on capture.
- **On-device VAD is mandatory** on the necklace and any ambient path. Streaming only detected speech, with a short pre-roll buffer, is what makes both the battery and the ASR bill survivable. See [Ambient capture](/docs/hardware/ambient-capture) for the cost math.

The practical rule: reserve direct WiFi for on-demand chat bursts, and route continuous ambient capture through the BLE relay. The plugged-in desk hub is exempt and stays on WiFi.

## Rough cost

These are ballpark parts costs, not a bill of materials.

| Device | Reference parts | Rough cost |
|---|---|---|
| Watch | LILYGO T-Watch S3, or DIY ESP32-S3 + round LCD | ~$15 to $43 |
| Necklace (audio) | ESP32-S3 + I2S mic + small LiPo | ~$25 to $35 |
| Necklace (camera) | XIAO ESP32-S3 Sense + OV2640 | ~$20 to $25 |
| Desk hub | ESP32-S3 + 7.5-inch e-ink + optional LCD + mic/speaker | ~$65 to $120 |

The recurring cost is not hardware or bandwidth, it is ASR, and it is dominated by how aggressively VAD trims silence. [Ambient capture](/docs/hardware/ambient-capture) has the numbers.
