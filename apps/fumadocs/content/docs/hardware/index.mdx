Ryu's hardware line is three physical devices that act as **thin clients to a node**: a watch, a necklace, and a desk hub. They capture audio and (optionally) camera, render chat, TTS, and glanceable dashboards, and run **no inference of their own**. Every device is built on the ESP32-S3, and every model call, transcription, and synthesis happens on a node you control, whether that node is your local desktop or a remote machine over Tailscale.

The thin-client principle is the whole design. The device is a microphone, a speaker, a small screen, and a radio. The node is the brain: it does ASR, runs the LLM, synthesizes TTS, and renders dashboards to images. This is the same split that already powers the rest of Ryu, so the hardware reuses Core's chat, voice, meetings, and dashboard pipelines rather than inventing new ones. See [Core vs Gateway](/docs/start-here/architecture/core-vs-gateway) for the wider version of this rule.

<Callout type="info">
  Status: Core is build-verified and the firmware is flash-ready, but the devices are not yet hardware-tested. Treat the firmware and pairing pages as first-bring-up guidance, not a proven appliance image.
</Callout>

A device reaches the node one of two ways. At home it speaks WiFi straight to the node (Mode B). Away from home it speaks BLE to your phone, and the phone relays to the node (Mode A). Both modes carry the **same** WebSocket protocol, the Ryu Hardware Protocol (RHP v1).
