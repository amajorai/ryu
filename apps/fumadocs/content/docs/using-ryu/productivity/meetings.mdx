title: "Meetings"
description: "Record a call, get a live transcript and AI notes, with automatic meeting detection."
---

Ryu records a call, transcribes it live, and generates structured AI notes - a summary, key points,
action items, and decisions. It can also detect when a meeting starts on its own and offer to begin
recording.

## Record a meeting

The **Meetings** section in the sidebar is your meeting list.

- Click **+** (or open **Meetings** from the command palette) to reach the record screen and start
  a meeting.
- A row shows a pulsing red dot while it is recording.
- Open a row to see that meeting's live transcript and, after finalizing, its notes.

Audio is captured from both your **microphone and system loopback** (so you get both sides of the
call), mixed down, and transcribed in chunks as the meeting runs.

## Automatic detection

Ryu can notice a meeting in progress by watching the operating system for **a process actively
using the microphone** - not which window is in focus. When it detects one, it surfaces a prompt so
you can start recording with one click.

Turn auto-detection on or off, and edit the list of apps that count as meeting apps, in
**Settings → Meetings**.

<Callout type="info">
  Mic capture and mic-in-use detection are **Windows-first**. On other platforms recording falls
  back to mic-only and detection is not yet available.
</Callout>

## Notes

When you **finalize** a meeting, Ryu generates the notes with a model call and writes them, together
with the transcript, as a document in an auto-created **Meetings** Space. That means you view and
edit notes in the same editor as the rest of your [Spaces](/docs/using-ryu/user-guide/spaces-memory),
and they are searchable for retrieval. The meeting detail's **Open notes** button takes you straight
to the document.

Choose the notes model, reasoning effort, and prompt in **Settings → Meetings**.
