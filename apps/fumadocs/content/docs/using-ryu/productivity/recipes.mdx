---
title: "Recorded Automations"
description: "Record a desktop task once and replay it as steps inside a workflow."
---

Ryu can record a desktop task once - your clicks, typing, and scrolls - and turn it into steps you
can replay. The idea is the one a frontier model records and a small model replays forever: capture
the task a single time, then run it deterministically.

## Record a task

Recording lives in the **Workflows** surface.

1. Open **Workflows** and click **Record a task**.
2. Describe the task, then start recording and perform it on screen.
3. Stop recording. Ryu mints the captured actions into a **visible workflow** - an `Input` node, one
   node per action, and an `Output` node - that you can then edit.

Each recorded action becomes a step node in the workflow's DAG, so a recorded automation is driven
by upstream data like any other workflow step. See [Workflows](/docs/core/workflows) for how to wire
nodes together.

<Callout type="warn">
  Desktop recording and replay are **Windows-first** and need the Ghost desktop-automation sidecar
  installed. The input capture is Windows-native; on other platforms recording and replay report a
  clear error until the sidecar is available, and replay needs a display.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info">
  Recording is backed by Ghost's recipe engine. Whole recipes can also be saved and replayed as a
  single `Recipe` node, in addition to the per-action step nodes a new in-canvas recording produces.
</Callout>
