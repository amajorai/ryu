---
title: "Agent Teams"
description: "Address several agents as one unit with a coordination strategy."
---

A **team** is a collection of agents plus a coordination strategy. You address the team as one
unit, and Ryu fans your turn out to its members and merges their replies into a single, attributed
answer. Teams are the counterpart to single-agent chat when you want several agents working
together on the same turn.

## Create a team

Teams live in their own **Teams** section in the sidebar.

1. Click **+** on the Teams section to create a team.
2. Pick the **coordination strategy** (see below).
3. Add members by **dragging an agent row onto the team**.

## Talk to a team

In chat, `@mention` the team by name in the composer to direct your turn to it. Every member's
reply is folded into one streamed answer, each block prefixed with the member's name so you can
see who said what. The user turn is persisted once, and the combined reply is attributed to the
team.

## Coordination strategies

Four strategies are built in:

- **Broadcast** - every member answers the turn independently.
- **Round-robin** - each member sees the previous members' replies and builds on them.
- **Debate / synthesis** - members answer, then the lead agent synthesizes one combined answer.
- **Router** - the lead agent classifies the turn and routes it to a single best member.

<Callout type="info">
  Council chat (`@mention` of individual agents in one thread) and teams are related but distinct:
  council chat targets agents one at a time, while a team is a named unit with a fixed roster and a
  strategy. A team can also be the route target for a [channel bot](/docs/gateway/channels).
</Callout>
