---
title: "Auto-capture a meeting and file the notes into a Space"
description: "Let Ryu detect a call, record it, and write structured notes into an editable, searchable Space."
---

You join calls and never get around to writing them up. This recipe lets Ryu notice the meeting on
its own, record both sides, and finalize a structured set of notes that land in a Space you can edit
and search later. It is for anyone on the desktop app who wants meeting notes without taking them by
hand.

## What you'll use

- [Meetings](/docs/using-ryu/productivity/meetings): record a call, get a live transcript and AI notes.
- [Meeting Notes (internals)](/docs/core/meeting-notes): how detection, capture, and finalize work.
- [Spaces and RAG](/docs/core/spaces-rag): where the finalized notes are stored and indexed.

<Callout type="warn">
  Mic capture and mic-in-use detection are Windows-first. On other platforms recording falls back to
  microphone-only and automatic detection is not yet available, so you start meetings manually there.
</Callout>

## Steps

<Steps>

<Step>

### Turn on auto-detection

Open **Settings → Meetings** and enable auto-detection. While you are there, review the list of apps
that count as meeting apps and add any you use. Detection does not watch which window is in focus: it
watches the operating system for a process actively holding the microphone, then matches that process
against your meeting-app list.

</Step>

<Step>

### Start a call and let the chip appear

Join a call as usual. When Ryu sees an app from your list grab the microphone, it surfaces a prompt
(on the island's suggestion chip) offering to start recording. Accept it to begin. You can also start
manually from the **Meetings** section in the sidebar by clicking **+** to reach the record screen.

</Step>

<Step>

### Record the meeting

Ryu captures your **microphone and the system loopback** together, so you get both sides of the call,
mixes them down, and transcribes the audio in roughly 20 second chunks as the call runs. The meeting
row shows a pulsing red dot while it records, and the transcript fills in live as each chunk is
transcribed.

</Step>

<Step>

### Finalize to generate the notes

When the call ends, **finalize** the meeting. Ryu turns the accumulated transcript into structured
notes with a single model call routed through the Gateway (the same path goals and double-check use,
so nothing is hardcoded). The notes come back in four parts:

```json
{
  "summary": "a short paragraph",
  "key_points": ["..."],
  "action_items": ["... ideally naming an owner ..."],
  "decisions": ["..."]
}
```

An empty transcript skips the model call entirely. You can choose the notes model, reasoning effort,
and prompt back in **Settings → Meetings**.

</Step>

<Step>

### Open the notes in the Spaces editor

On finalize, Ryu writes the notes and transcript as a document into an auto-created, hidden
**Meetings** Space. The meeting detail's **Open notes** button takes you straight to that document in
the standard [Spaces editor](/docs/using-ryu/user-guide/spaces-memory), so you can edit it like any
other doc. Because it lives in a Space, it is RAG-indexed and searchable for retrieval. A second
meeting reuses the same Space, so over time you build one Space holding all your meeting notes.

</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
  The chunked transcription, note generation, and Spaces paths were exercised against a live Core, but
  the loopback capture and the registry mic-in-use detection are not yet runtime-verified: they need a
  real call on a Windows display.
</Callout>
