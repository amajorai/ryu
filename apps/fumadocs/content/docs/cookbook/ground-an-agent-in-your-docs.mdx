---
title: "Ground an agent in your own documents"
description: "Build a Space from your files, attach it to an agent, and let it answer from your content with RAG."
---

You want an agent that answers from *your* documents, a handbook, a codebase's docs, a folder of
notes, not just its training data. This recipe builds a Space (a vector store), fills it with your
content, and attaches it to an agent so its answers are grounded in what you put there. It is for
anyone who wants a private, retrieval-backed assistant over their own material.

## What you'll use

- [Spaces & Memory](/docs/using-ryu/user-guide/spaces-memory): create a Space and attach it to an agent.
- [Spaces & RAG](/docs/core/spaces-rag): how retrieval is chunked, embedded, and scored.

## Steps

<Steps>

<Step>

### Create a Space

A Space is a vector store backed by sqlite-vec inside Core. Open the **Spaces** page and create one,
giving it a name.

<TryInRyu page="spaces" />

Over the API that is `POST /api/spaces`.

</Step>

<Step>

### Add your documents

Add content to the Space. Each document is split into chunks and embedded on ingest, so it is
retrieved by meaning, not just keyword:

```http
POST /api/spaces/:id/documents
```

You can also start a blank page and write notes directly in the editor; saving re-embeds it so
retrieval stays current. The default embeddings run locally (the `llamacpp-embed` sidecar that
onboarding sets up), so your documents are embedded on your machine.

</Step>

<Step>

### Attach the Space to an agent

Attach the Space from the agent's edit page. Once attached, the agent retrieves the most relevant
chunks from it on each turn and answers grounded in them. Under the hood this is the unified
retrieval path (`POST /api/retrieval/search`), which spans Spaces and encrypted memory together and
ranks by similarity.

</Step>

<Step>

### Ask it

Chat with the agent and ask something only your documents would answer. It pulls the closest chunks
from the Space and writes its reply from them.

<TryInRyu page="chat" />

</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
Retrieval is private by default: the embedding model runs locally, and the Space lives in Core on
your machine. Nothing about your documents is sent to a provider just to index them.
</Callout>
