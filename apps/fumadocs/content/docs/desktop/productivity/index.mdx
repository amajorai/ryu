Beyond chat, Ryu ships a set of assistant features that do work for you in the background - taking
meeting notes, watching websites for changes, and replaying recorded desktop actions as workflow
steps.

## What's here

- **[Meetings](/docs/desktop/productivity/meetings)** - record a call, get a live transcript and
  AI notes, with automatic detection of an in-progress meeting.
- **[Monitors](/docs/desktop/productivity/monitors)** - watch a URL on a schedule and get
  alerted on price, stock, content, keyword, or uptime changes.
- **[Recorded Automations](/docs/desktop/productivity/recipes)** - record a desktop task once and
  replay it as steps inside a workflow.
- **[Timeline](#timeline)** - scrub a visual history of your screen and activity, with the nearest
  screenshot shown at the playhead.

<Callout type="info">
  Meeting capture and desktop recording are Windows-first today - they rely on the Shadow and Ghost
  sensors, which are Windows-native. The notes, monitors, and workflow surfaces themselves work
  anywhere Core runs.
</Callout>

## Timeline

The **Timeline** is a scrubbable visual history of what was on your screen. Open it to drag a
playhead across a time range and see the nearest recorded screenshot at each moment, alongside the
active app, window title, and a list of nearby captured events grouped into lanes (screen, input,
window, audio, clipboard, filesystem, git, terminal, and more).

The page (`apps/desktop/src/pages/TimelinePage.tsx`) reads timeline events from the Shadow sidecar's
`GET /timeline` endpoint and shows the screenshot for the playhead via `frameUrl()` in
`apps/desktop/src/lib/api/shadow.ts`, which points an `<img>` at Shadow's `GET /frame?ts=` route. The
range picker (15 minutes, 1 hour, 6 hours, 24 hours) lives in the window titlebar, and the transport
bar adds play, step, jump-to-now, zoom, and 1x/10x/60x playback speeds.

### How the screenshots are recorded

Shadow records screen **keyframes as JPEGs** through a pure-Rust path (the `image` crate, no ffmpeg)
in `apps/shadow/src/video/mod.rs` (`VideoEncoder::save_keyframe`). Frames are written on each changed
frame at the encoder's 0.5 fps target to `<data>/media/keyframes/display-<id>/<ts>.jpg` and recorded
in Shadow's index. Because the keyframe path does not depend on the optional `video` cargo feature,
the timeline shows real screenshots out of the box. Wide frames are downscaled (max width 1280 px)
to keep the JPEGs small, and old keyframes are pruned by age.

Frame capture is **consent-gated**: it runs only when frame recording is enabled, capture is not
paused, and the app passes the allowlist. You toggle frame recording, pause, and the app allowlist
through Shadow's `POST /capture/control` route, surfaced in the desktop **Settings → Shadow** tab
(with a compact mirror in **Settings → Island**).

<Callout type="warn">
  The Timeline is **Windows-first** and not yet exercised end to end. It needs Shadow's `:3030`
  server running and consent-gated frame capture enabled. When Shadow is unreachable the page shows
  a "Shadow is not running" state; when frame capture is off, paused, or nothing was recorded near a
  moment, the playhead shows a "No frame recorded" placeholder.
</Callout>
