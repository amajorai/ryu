title: "Teams"
description: "Address several agents as one unit with a coordination strategy."
---

A **team** is a collection of agents plus a coordination strategy. You address the team as one
unit, and Ryu fans your turn out to its members and merges their replies into a single, attributed
answer. Teams are the counterpart to single-agent chat when you want several agents working
together on the same turn.

## Create a team

Teams live in their own **Teams** section in the sidebar.

1. Click **+** on the Teams section to create a team.
2. Pick the **coordination strategy** (see below).
3. Add members by **dragging an agent row onto the team**.

## Talk to a team

There are two ways to direct a turn to a team:

- **Composer target picker** - Click the agent picker in the composer and select a team from the
  list. Teams appear alongside agents; selecting one sets it as the persistent target for the
  conversation. The selection is stored as a `team:` prefix on the id and sent as `team_id` on the
  request. `team_id` takes precedence over `agent_id`, so the whole turn fans out to the team.
- **`@mention`** - Type `@teamname` in the composer for a one-off team mention within a
  conversation where you are otherwise talking to a single agent.

Every member's reply is folded into one streamed answer, each block prefixed with the member's
name so you can see who said what. The user turn is persisted once, and the combined reply is
attributed to the team.

## Coordination strategies

Four strategies are built in:

- **Broadcast** - every member answers the turn independently. No member sees another's reply.
  This is the default.
- **Round-robin** - each member answers in order, seeing the prior members' replies folded into
  its prompt.
- **Debate / synthesis** - members answer independently (round one), then the lead agent reads
  all replies and produces one merged answer.
- **Router** - the lead agent classifies the turn and routes it to a single best-suited member.

For `debate-synthesis` and `router`, the lead is the agent in the `lead_agent_id` field; it
falls back to the first member when unset.

<Callout type="info">
  Council chat (`@mention` of individual agents in one thread) and teams are related but distinct:
  council chat targets agents one at a time, while a team is a named unit with a fixed roster and a
  strategy.
</Callout>

## Expose a team via a channel bot

A team can be the route target for a channel bot (Telegram, Slack, WhatsApp, Discord) configured
in **Gateway settings**. When a channel is bound to a team, each inbound message is dispatched by
Core through `run_team_reply_text` - the non-streaming sibling of the in-app path that fans out to
the team's members under the same coordination strategy and returns one combined, attributed reply.

The **Routes to** picker in the Gateway channels dialog lists agents and teams. Selecting a team
stores it with a `team:` sentinel and persists it as `teamId` on the channel config. Each sender
gets a persistent conversation in Core's `conversations.db`, so history, memory, and tools all
apply just as they do in the desktop.

See [Channel Bots](/docs/gateway/channels) for full setup instructions.
